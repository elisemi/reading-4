The internet is like rhizomes. The internet known also as “the web” is more similar to rhizomes
than a web. Rhizomes are stems that shoot into the ground and continuously grow roots under-
ground that shoot out horizontally. Ginger is one example of a rhizome. Spider webs connect to
each other and are consistent throughout. Rhizomes are uncontrollable and unconnected even though they might overlap. This describes the internet because the internet is much less ordered
and not everything is connected to each other. The internet is also unpredictable. A rhizome is also
a concept in philosophy developed by philosophers Deleuze and Guattari. It describes the idea
that existence does not come from one central point. This can also be applied to the internet.
